## 🧪 Overview

This repository contains the research paper and supporting information for my cybersecurity experiment titled **"Email Scam Detection in the Real World."** The study evaluates phishing email awareness among non-IT employees in a real-world setting — specifically, a 4-star hotel in Bosnia and Herzegovina.

> 📝 This research was conducted as part of my final-year university project in the field of cybersecurity.

The experiment involved sending a simulated phishing email to hotel receptionists to observe behavioral responses and gauge cybersecurity awareness levels outside the IT sector.

---

## 🧠 Abstract

Phishing remains one of the most successful forms of social engineering. This study investigates phishing awareness among non-IT hotel reception staff by sending them a realistic simulated email that mimicked a Booking.com cancellation notice.

> **Results:**  
> - 4 out of 6 employees clicked the phishing link  
> - 3 of them attempted to log in using their real credentials  
> - Only 2 employees correctly identified the email as suspicious

These results demonstrate the urgent need for basic cybersecurity education even in non-technical roles.

---

## 🛠️ Methodology

- ✅ **Participants:** 6 hotel reception employees with no IT background  
- ✅ **Phishing Simulation:** Fake Booking.com email with a disguised domain  
- ✅ **Monitoring:** Clicks recorded via a neutral landing page  
- ✅ **Ethics:** Experiment approved by hotel management and IT department  
- ✅ **Post-Analysis:** Interviews conducted to understand reasoning

---

## 📊 Results Summary

| Behavior                       | Count |
|-------------------------------|-------|
| Clicked phishing link         | 4     |
| Attempted login with password | 3     |
| Ignored or flagged email      | 2     |

These findings reveal a critical vulnerability in real-world service environments, highlighting the need for awareness training.

---

## 🔐 Key Takeaways

- Non-IT staff are highly vulnerable to phishing attempts  
- Job experience correlates with phishing awareness  
- Urgent messaging and brand trust increase susceptibility  
- Regular simulations and awareness programs are essential  
- Organizations must train **all staff**, not just technical teams
